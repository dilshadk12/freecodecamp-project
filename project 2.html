Eliud Kipchoge

Kenyan long-distance runner (track and field)

Here's a time line of Eliud Kipchoge's life:

  • 1984- Born in Kenya
  • 1999- Graduated from High School but did not run as a profession.He ran three kilometers to scchool on a daily basis.
  • 2002- He won at kenyan trials of the 2002 IAAF World Cross Countary Championship junior race.
  • 2004- Kipchoge won a bronze medal at the 5000 m final at the Athens Olympics.
  • 2008- He made his half marathon debut in the Lille Half Marathon.Kipchoge earned a third place with the timing of 59.25.
  • 2012- He switched to road running in 2012 and made the second-fastest half marathon debut ever,at 59.25.He went on to become series champion a record five times- for 2016,2017,2018,2019 and 2022.
  • 2013- Making his marathone debut in April, he demonstrated a smooth transition to a longer distance by taking the Hamburg Marathone title with a run of 2:05:30 hours.
  • 2014- On 16 September ,Lipchoge won the 2018 Berlin Marathon in a time of 2:01:39, breaking the previous world record by 1 minute and 18 seconds. It was the greatest improvement in a marathon world record time since 1967.
  • 2016- During the Rio Summer Olympics, Kipchoge gained a gold medal in the marathon event.The winning gap between Kipchoge and the Runner-Up Lilesa by70seconds was the largest victory margin since the 1972 Olympic marathon.
  • 2019- Kipchoge named IAAF World Athele of the year.
  • 2019- In Oct he ran 4.4 laps of the Hauptallee in 1:59:40, becoming the first person in recorded history to break the two-hour barrier over a marathon distance.
  • 2021- On 20 January, Kipchoge announced his desire to win all 6 Abbott World Marathons Majors(he had already won 3, the london,Berlin,and Chicago marathon,by that time).Due to covid restriction the 2021 Tokyo marathone postponed to 2022.He won Tokyo Marathon with a time of 2:02:40.
